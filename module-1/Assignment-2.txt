Immutable Ledger Case Study

Assignment Overview:
In this assignment, you will investigate a real-world case where the use of an immutable ledger (such as a blockchain) could have potentially prevented fraud, tampering, or data manipulation. Understanding the practical applications of immutable ledgers is crucial in today's data-driven world, where security and integrity are paramount.

Assignment Instructions:

Task 1: Case Selection (15 minutes)

Choose a real-world case of fraud, tampering, or data manipulation that has occurred in recent years. It could be in any industry, such as finance, healthcare, supply chain, or government.
Task 2: Research (1-1.5 hours)
2. Conduct in-depth research on the chosen case. Utilize reputable sources such as news articles, reports, legal documents, or academic papers to gather information about the incident.

Task 3: Report (45 minutes - 1 hour)
3. Write a report that includes the following elements:

Introduction (10%):

Provide an introduction to the report, including the selected case and its relevance to the assignment.
Case Description (20%):

Describe the chosen case in detail. Explain what happened, who was involved, and the consequences of the fraud, tampering, or data manipulation.
Use of Immutable Ledger (30%):

Explain how the use of an immutable ledger, such as a blockchain, could have prevented or mitigated the issues described in the case.
Discuss the specific features and properties of an immutable ledger that would have been beneficial in this context.
Recommendations (30%):

Provide recommendations on how the industry or organization involved in the case could implement an immutable ledger to enhance security and integrity.
Consider the practical challenges and considerations that should be addressed when implementing such a solution.
Conclusion (10%):

Summarize the key findings of your case study.
Emphasize the importance of immutable ledgers in preventing fraud and ensuring data integrity.
Submission Guidelines:

Your report should be well-organized and between 800 to 1,000 words in length.
Properly cite and reference any sources you use.
Use clear headings and subheadings to structure your report.
Follow a standard citation style (e.g., APA, MLA, or Chicago) for your references.
Grading Criteria:
Your assignment will be graded based on the following criteria:

Depth of understanding of the chosen case.
Clarity and organization of the report.
Use of relevant examples and evidence to support your arguments.
Practical and well-thought-out recommendations for using immutable ledgers.
